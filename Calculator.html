Background Information

The concept of the carbon footprint calculator has picked up steam in the last few years and is a classic, if not THE original example of greenwashing by a corporate giant.

The origin of the carbon footprint was seen in the 1990s, through the introduction of the concept of Ecological Footprint in Vancouver by Wackernagel and Rees. The ecological footprint was defined as the total area of land required to sustain an urban region, where more economically developed Western nations often tend to consume more than their fair share of this land. This paper was written as an analysis of how economic theory fails to account for ecology, and how models are based on “reductionist and deterministic assumptions about resources, people, firms and technology” (Wackernagel, 1998).

This concept was largely unnoticed until 2004 when British oil and gas conglomerate British Petroleum hired advertising agency Ogilvy and Mather to promote the concept of the 'Carbon Footprint' by marketing it with an online Carbon Footprint Calculator which successfully misinformed consumers about their individual consumptions, and lifestyle choices leading to climate change and the destruction of ecosystems worldwide.
